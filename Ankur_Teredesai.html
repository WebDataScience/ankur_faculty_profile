Ankur Teredesai

Prof. Teredesai actively mentors graduate and undergraduate students, is engaged in developing new approaches to enhance participation of non-traditional students to computing, and has been actively developing new courses and programs in computing; most recently the Information Technology and Systems degree program at University of Washington Tacoma.

Prof. Teredesai is an Associate Professor and Graduate Program Coordinator at the Institute of Technology, University of Washington Tacoma. He obtained a doctorate from SUNY, University at Buffalo. Apart from his academic appointments at RIT and UWT, he has significant industrial experience and held various positions at C-DAC Pune, Microsoft, and IBM T.J. Watson Labs. Prof. Teredesai has helped design and develop several deployed data science solutions in various verticals including internet advertising, social networks and handwritten zip code recognizers. His other applied research contributions include risk prediction for healthcare data mining, social recommendations, distributed algorithms for data mining, novelty detection and volume estimation from video streams, to name a few. He directs the activities for the Center for Web and Data Science and serves as the Information Officer for ACM SIGKDD (Special Interest Group in Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining).
